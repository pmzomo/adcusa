Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ADCUSA a registered nonprofit?

Yes. ADCUSA is a federally recognized 501(c)(3) public charity (EIN: 46-4204919). All donations are tax-deductible to the fullest extent permitted by law. We were founded in 2015 and are classified under NTEE code A23 for Cultural and Ethnic Awareness.

How do I donate to ADCUSA?

You can donate online via PayPal on our Get Involved page. Alternatively, you may mail a check payable to ADCUSA at P.O. Box 12616 RTP, Durham, NC 27709. All donations receive a tax-deductible receipt.

Do I need to be African to join or volunteer?

Not at all. While ADCUSA focuses on the African diaspora community, we welcome allies, supporters, and volunteers of all backgrounds who share our vision of unity and development. Our programs benefit entire communities, not just one group.

How can my organization partner with ADCUSA?

We partner with universities, hospitals, businesses, government agencies, and other nonprofits. Partnership can involve co-hosting events, providing in-kind support, sponsoring programs, or contributing professional expertise. Contact us via this page to start the conversation.

Where does ADCUSA operate?

ADCUSA is based in Durham, North Carolina (Research Triangle area) and is active in 5+ U.S. states. We also have a broader vision for programs that support development across Africa, working to connect the diaspora with opportunities on both continents.

How can I get a receipt for my donation?

Online donations via PayPal receive an automatic receipt. For mailed checks, contact us at drmwalalip@yahoo.com and we will send you a formal donation receipt with our EIN (46-4204919) for tax purposes.
